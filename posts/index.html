Is the Big10 truly taking over the SEC’s spot as the best conference?

Let’s look at last years stats to figure that out.

The first stats that I want to take a look at is on the offensive side of the ball. While defense does win championships, you need to score to win. I am going to be looking at each SEC and Big 10’s rushing and passing game from last year.

BIGSEC <- c("Nebraska", "Iowa", "Minnesota", "Illinois", "Wisconsin", "Michigan", "Michigan St", "Ohio State", "Purdue", "Indiana", "Maryland", "Rutgers", "Penn State", "Northwestern", "Alabama", "Arkansas", "Auburn", "Florida", "Georgia", "Kentucky", "LSU", "Mississippi State", "Missouri", "Oklahoma", "Ole Miss", "South Carolina", "Tennessee", "Texas", "Texas A&M", "Vanderbilt")

DTab <- football|>
  filter(Team %in% BIGSEC)|>
  select(Team, 
         DefFirstDownTotal, 
         DefTotalTurnovers,
         DefRushingTD, 
         DefPassingTD)|>
  mutate_at(vars(-Team), as.numeric)|>
  group_by(Team)|>
  summarize(
    TotalFirstDowns = sum(DefFirstDownTotal),
    TotalTOs = sum(DefTotalTurnovers),
    RushTD = sum(DefRushingTD),
    PassTD = sum(DefPassingTD)
  )|>
  mutate(TotalTD = RushTD + PassTD
         )|>
  select(Team, TotalTOs, TotalFirstDowns, TotalTD)|>
  arrange(TotalTD)
